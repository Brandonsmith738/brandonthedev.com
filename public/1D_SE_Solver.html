We want to find the eigenvectors that satisfy: $$ \hat{H} \psi = E \psi $$ Where $\hat{H}=\frac{\hat{P}^2}{2m}+V(x)$ $$ \frac{\hat{P}^2}{2m}\psi+V(x)\psi = E\psi $$ $$ \frac{-\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\partial^2 \psi}{\partial x^2} +V(x)\psi = E\psi $$ $$ -\frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial^2 \psi}{\partial x^2} +mV(x)\psi = mE\psi $$ I want to make this dimensionless, substitute: $\epsilon=x/L$ $$ -\frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial^2 \psi}{\partial x^2} +mL^2V(\epsilon)\psi = mL^2E\psi $$ and we do a finite difference for second derivatives:

After plugging in the finite difference for second derivatives and then rearranging: $$ -\frac{\psi_N}{2\Delta\epsilon^2}+(\frac{1}{\Delta\epsilon^2}+mL^2V(\epsilon)_{N-1})\psi_{N-1}-\frac{\psi_{N-2}}{2\Delta\epsilon^2}=mL^2E\psi_{N-1}$$ This becomes a tridiagonal matrix and ultimately an eigen problem. We can then solve the eigen problem to solve the differential equation and thus find $\psi_N$ and $E_N$

Define: $\Delta \epsilon=\frac{1}{N}$
